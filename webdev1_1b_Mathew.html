Chicken Adobo

What is Chicken Adobo?

A dish and cooking process native to the Philippines,
adobo refers to the method of marinating meat, seafood, or vegetables
(pretty much anything!) in a combination of soy sauce and vinegar.
This marinade also includes other herbs and flavorings like garlic,
bay leaves, and whole peppercorns.

Cooking food in vinegar is no foreign concept to us Filipinos.
In pre-colonial times, our ancestors used to cook seafood in vinegar
in order to preserve their freshness. Many regard adobo as a spin on kinilaw,
which is another traditional cooking method. Kinilaw refers mainly to cooking
raw seafood in vinegar and spices. Another similar process is paksiw,
which utilizes meat broth in vinegar and spices.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup of white vinegar
  • 1/4 cup of soy sauce
  • 1 whole garlic bulb,smashed and peeled
  • 2 teespoon kosher salt
  • 1 teaspoon coarsly ground pepper
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 2 pounds bone-in chicken thighs or drumsticks
  • 1 table spoon canola oil
  • 1 cup of water

Steps

  1. In a shallow dish, add canola oil
  2. Add the 1 whole garlic bulb, smashed and peeled
  3. Add the 2 pounds chicken thighs
  4. Add the 1/4 cup of soy sauce
  5. cover and wait for 2 minutes
  6. Add the 2 teaspoons of kosher salt
  7. Add the 1 teaspoon of coarsely ground pepper
  8. Add the 1 cup white vinegar
  9. Add 1 cup of water
  10. cover and simmer for 1 minute
  11. Add the 1 bay leaf
  12. Add simmer for 4 minutes with low fire
